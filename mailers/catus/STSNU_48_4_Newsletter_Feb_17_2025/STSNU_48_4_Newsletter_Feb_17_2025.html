Prof. Ki-Tae Kim and team published a new study where they proposed a novel extraction strategy for simultaneous lipidomics and metabolomics analysis of perfluorooctanesulfonate (PFOS)-induced neurotoxicity in zebrafish embryos. Their findings advance our understanding of toxicity mechanisms of chemicals like per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, expediting environmental toxicity research.

A SEOULTECH research team led by Prof. Dongwook Han, developed an innovative technology to improve the Lithium-ion battery technology. They engineered a Li-vacant topotactic subsurface with a protective K2CO3 surface layer on LiNi0.5Mn1.5O4 cathodes, which enhanced the stability, longevity, and performance of Li-ion batteries. This breakthrough can revolutionize energy solutions globally.
Read more
Under the guidance of Prof. Hyungjoon Seo, SEOULTECH researchers developed a novel autonomous geological assessment tool based on a methodology called Roughness-CANUPO-Dip-Facet (R-C-D-F), that uses machine learning for assessments of rock faces. This fully autonomous approach is a key step towards enhanced precision and safety in large construction projects, such as tunnels and mines, reducing human error and improving efficiency in geological data processing.
Read more
In a new breakthrough study, Prof. Insup Noh and team have developed a novel bioink made from Kombucha SCOBY-derived nanocellulose, which acts as a scaffold to grow cells for tissue repair. Using a handheld biopen, the bioink can be directly applied to the damaged areas. This makes it ideal for direct in vivo tissue engineering of wounds and complex defects, particularly in emergency and first-aid settings.

Introducing the Environmental Technology Research
Institute at SEOULTECH
Established in 1990, the Environmental Technology Research Institute conducts comprehensive academic research on tech-driven environmental conservation, contributing to the local community, with collaborations with the Seoul Green Environment Regional Center and the Environmental Industry Consortium Center
Our cutting-edge technology helps us provide consultation on environmental safety, environmental safety management systems, and offer pre-diagnostic analysis of sustainability and establishment strategy
With international collaborative research, our experts integrate the fields of air, water, soil assessment, environmental toxicity research, and surveillance systems using big data

At our Department of Civil Engineering, we cater to create safe and environment-friendly civil structures through the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure.
Our broad-based, vocational programme covers all the aspects of a civil engineer’s education, along with its contextual application.
Students are introduced to structural analysis and design, materials, geotechnical engineering, water supply, sanitation, construction management, and its digitization.
With an understanding of the climate crisis and the ongoing biodiversity crisis, we equip our students with how civil engineers can participate in addressing these global challenges.
With an opportunity for industrial feedback, encouragement for internship with approved companies/organizations, and rigorous training for critical thinking, collaboration, communication, and creativity, our civil engineering graduates are in great demand.
