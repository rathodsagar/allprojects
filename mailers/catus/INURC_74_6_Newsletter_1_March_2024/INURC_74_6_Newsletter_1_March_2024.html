Prominent Research
 
How Climate Change Affects Marine Microbes and Reshapes Ocean Methane and Nitrous Oxide Cycles
 
Climate change impacts alter the delicate marine microbial population which plays a crucial role in greenhouse gas production. Scientists at Incheon National University have investigated how climate change influences marine microbes. Their findings help raise awareness about climate change severity and the importance of ocean resources.
Unraveling prokaryotic diversity distribution and functional pattern on nitrogen and methane cycling in the subtropical Western North Pacific Ocean, Marine Pollution Bulletin, 196, 115569.

A New Detection System for Autonomous Vehicles Powered by Deep Learning
 
A team of researchers have developed a novel IoT enabled deep learning-based end-to-end 3D object detection system for autonomous driving. It enhances detection in adverse conditions, addressing limitations in current models. This study may help autonomous vehicles become mainstream.
A Smart IoT Enabled End-to-End 3D Object Detection System for Autonomous Vehicles, IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, vol. 24, no. 11, pp. 13078–13087, Nov 2023.

A New and Improved Separator for Preventing Battery Short-Circuit
 
Polyolefin separators often experience mechanical and thermal failure, leading to short circuits in batteries. Now, researchers have demonstrated how PPS can be modified with polymer grafting and SiO2 nanoparticle coating. This method can improve the electrochemical performance and safety of lithium batteries.
Ultra-thin SiO2 nanoparticle layered separators by a surface multi-functionalization strategy for Li-metal batteries: Highly enhanced Li-dendrite resistance and thermal properties. Energy Storage Materials, 65, 103135.

Enhancing Lifespan of Fuel Cells with Fatigue-Resistant Electrolyte Membranes
 
Hydrogen fuel cells often run unsteadily, causing the membrane to swell and contract, which ultimately leads to fatigue crack growth. This study describes an approach to developing a fatigue-resistant polymer electrolyte membrane by forming an interpenetrating network of plastic electrolyte and rubber.
Fatigue-Resistant Polymer Electrolyte Membranes for Fuel Cells, Advanced Materials, 2023, 2308288.

Exploring the Transition: Moving Real-Time Services from Web 2.0 to Web 3.0
 
Web 3.0 presents numerous advantages; however, some technologies in the Web 3.0 stack are unsuitable for real-time applications. In this study, the researchers propose an end-to-end system architecture designed for Web 3.0 real-time services, aiming to prevent the degradation of service quality.

Predicting the Learning Performance of Construction Workers in Virtual Reality (VR)-Based Safety Training
 
Korea Occupational Safety and Health Agency (KOSHA) has been offering daily workers virtual VR-based construction safety content as part of their educational training initiatives. However, the existing methods lack immediate feedback from participants. Now, researchers propose a new approach that uses real-time biometric data to improve personalized safety training.
Forecasting personal learning performance in virtual reality-based construction safety training using biometric responses, Automation in Construction, 156, 105115.

A Novel Tissue Adhesive That Improves Wound Healing
 
Conventional techniques for wound management, like sutures and stapling, have several drawbacks. In a recent study, researchers developed a new type of gelatin-based tissue adhesive hydrogel. This hydrogel, through a reaction mediated by calcium peroxide locally generates oxygen, thereby enhancing material adhesiveness and improving the healing process.
Oxygen-generating tissue adhesives via CaO2-mediated oxygen generation and in situ catechol oxidation for wound management, Composites Part B: Engineering, 266, 110951.

Organic–Inorganic Hybrid Gas Sensors with Enhanced Durability and Sensitivity
 
Industrial environments carry the potential threat of exposure to hazardous gases. Currently available gas sensors are expensive and complex, restricting their widespread application. In a recent study, researchers from Korea developed an organic–inorganic hybrid semiconductor gas sensor that exhibited remarkable performance and durability while being cost-effective. This technology could pave the way for convenient gas sensing devices.
Polymeric interfacial engineering approach to perovskite-functionalized organic transistor-type gas sensors. Chemical Engineering Journal, 473, 145482.

Prominent Research
 
Can Job Autonomy Drive Change in Public Organizations?
 
Job autonomy is thought to be a critical condition for employee change-oriented behavior. Using survey data from Korean government workers, however, this study shows that job autonomy impacts change-oriented behavior only when role ambiguity is low and procedural justice is high. The results are relevant to public service performance.
Job Autonomy, Role Ambiguity, and Procedural Justice: A Multi-Conditional Process Model of Change-Oriented Organizational Citizenship Behavior in Public Organizations. Review of Public Personnel Administration, 0(0).
