CNU's Outstanding Research Achievements in 2024
Role of CSR and Online Shopping Ease in Shaping Global Consumer Behavior
 
Keywords: Corporate Social Responsibility, Global Product Purchases, Online Purchasability
Prof. Ha Kyung Lee from CNU and his team explored how corporate social responsibility (CSR) and the ease of online shopping influences global consumer behavior, focusing on South Korea and Japan. The study found that CSR initiatives combined with user-friendly e-commerce platforms boost purchase frequency, with Japanese consumers responding more strongly to CSR efforts.

Power-Free Color-Changing Strain Sensor
 
Keywords: Nanoparticles, Health Monitoring, Infrastructure
CNU researchers have developed a power-free mechanochromic strain sensor that changes color in response to mechanical stress. Made from magnetic nanoparticles and flexible materials, it offers versatile applications in health monitoring, motion tracking, and infrastructure safety. The sensor’s color shift is reversible and stable, providing a durable, user-friendly solution for real-time monitoring.

Detecting Fecal Contamination Using Viral DNA for Better Hygiene Standards
 
Keywords: Public Health, Hygiene, Viral DNA
Scientists from CNU and Gyeongsang National University developed a new method to detect fecal contamination in water using viral DNA markers. Focusing on CrAss-like phages, which are found exclusively in human feces, this method improves the ability to identify human fecal contamination in water, potentially enhancing hygiene standards and mitigating public health risks.

Enhancing CO2 Reduction with Recyclable Copper-Zinc Electrodes
 
Keywords: CO2 Reduction, Copper-Zinc Electrodes, Electrochemistry
A research team at CNU explored the use of copper-zinc (CuZn) electrodes for electrochemical CO2 reduction (EC CO2R). The study demonstrates that CuZn electrodes provide enhanced stability and catalytic efficiency, outperforming single-metal alternatives. Notably, these electrodes maintain high performance over multiple recycling cycles, offering a promising solution for CO2 reduction technologies.

Rapid Water Quality Monitoring Chip for Antibiotic Detection
 
Keywords: Antibiotic Detection, Wastewater, Ecosystem
CNU researchers developed a microfluidic sensor, μTMP-chip, for rapid on-site detection of trimethoprim (TMP), an antibiotic commonly found in wastewater. This portable, disposable sensor provides an effective solution for monitoring environmental antibiotic contamination, contributing to efforts against antimicrobial resistance and protecting ecosystems.

Sustainable Edible Biofilm for Extending the Shelf Life of Fruits
 
Keywords: Food Waste, Sustainability, Antibacterial, Biofilm
Researchers at CNU developed an eco-friendly chitosan-gallic acid (CS-GA) biofilm to extend the shelf life of perishable fruits. This natural polysaccharide-based coating enhances mechanical strength, antioxidant properties, and antibacterial activity, offering a viable alternative to synthetic packaging. This innovation presents a sustainable solution to reduce food waste and supports global efforts to minimize environmental impact.
