The potential of clean energy markets as a hedging tool
 
Climate change has prompted governments worldwide to implement policies that prioritize clean energy. Clean energy assets have a distinct investment advantage: They can offer profit and stability, particularly in turbulent markets. In a study published in Energy Economics, Professor Sang Hoon Kang from the School of Business, Pusan National University, and his team investigated the relationship between clean energy markets and global stock markets, revealing crucial insights for leveraging clean energy assets as hedging tools in volatile market environments.

Minimizing Risk in Highly Volatile Markets with Perpetual American Strangle Options 
Perpetual American Strangle Options (PASOs) offer a flexible investment strategy that allows investors to buy or sell options at any time without an expiration date, minimizing risk during volatile market conditions. A research team led by Professor Ji-Hun Yun from the Department of Mathematics developed a pricing formula for PASOs using a stochastic volatility model. This study provides investors and financial institutions with enhanced tools and strategies to manage risk and maximize profits in turbulent markets.

Artificial Intelligence Unlocks Enhanced Sound-Damping Materials
Ventilated acoustic resonator (VAR), a novel acoustic metamaterial, enables simultaneous noise reduction and ventilation for urban areas, but conventional design techniques fall short due to its complex geometry. Associate Professor Sang Min Park from the School of Mechanical Engineering, along with his team, developed a new deep-learning-based method for designing enhanced VARs, which also reduces computational costs. This strategy can be used for designing other mechanical structures as well.

Lego-Like Construction for Fast and Accurate Analysis of Composite Laminates
Composite laminates have numerous applications in various industries. However, traditional three-dimensional finite element analysis methods are impractical for their simulation and design. Professor Kyunghoon "K" Lee and Shinseong Kang, from the Department of Aerospace Engineering, developed an innovative, “Lego-like” construction method that enables highly flexible, real-time, fast, and accurate analysis of composite laminates.

AI-Assisted Backscatter Communication Technique Enables Low-Power Internet of Things Communication
For widespread adoption of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies, Associate Professor Sangkil Kim from the Department of Electronics Engineering developed a new low-power backscatter communication system using transfer learning. This system significantly improves both spectral and energy efficiency compared to traditional methods, marking a significant step for IoT communications.

Revolutionizing E-Nose Technology with Time-Resolved Hyperspectral Analysis 
Colorimetric sensor-based electronic nose classifies gaseous molecules for applications, like health and environment monitoring. However, conventional colorimetric systems use red, green, and blue (RGB) sensors, which cannot capture the complete spectral response of the system. Associate Professor Seungchul Kim from the Department of Optics and Mechatronics Engineering and Professor Jin-Woo Oh from the Department of Nano Fusion Technology, and their team have developed a novel time-resolved hyperspectral data set that significantly improves classification accuracy compared to the RGB model.
