IKMC2024: A Grand Confluence of Innovation
Our annual conference, IKMC2024, successfully captured the essence of our epic 25-year journey and our vision for the next symphony of innovation and discovery. The event brought together distinguished speakers, industry leaders, and entrepreneurs to share insights and inspire the next wave of advancements in deep science. Highlights of the conference included awards and recognitions, the ‘Distinguished Lecture’ series, a startup expo, the CXO Conclave, and the launch of several key initiatives and partnerships.
A Transformative Leadership Transition at IKP
We underwent a significant transformation this year, with our former Chief Executive Officer, Deepanwita Chattopadhyay, handing over the baton to Dr. Sanjay Sarin. With 25+ years of global health leadership, Dr. Sarin is set to lead IKP into a new era of innovation and impact. While Ms. Chattopadhyay continues to guide us as Chairman and Managing Director, her legacy remains the cornerstone of IKP’s success. 
Pioneering Biomanufacturing at IKP
We announced the launch of a cutting-edge Biomanufacturing Hub to advance cell and gene therapy. In partnership with leading startups, this initiative aims to drive healthcare innovation and improve global accessibility. Led by Dr. Viswanadham Duppatla, this facility will mark a transformative step in positioning India as a global leader in biomanufacturing.
Launch of Centre for Smart Protein and Sustainable Materials Innovation
IKP launched India’s leading Centre for Smart Protein and Sustainable Materials Innovation at its EDEN facility in Bengaluru, in partnership with Good Food Institute India, fostering cutting-edge research and startups in sustainable protein solutions. At the launch, we also signed key MoUs with AIC Jyothi Institute of Technology, Bühler Group, and the Transdisciplinary University to drive innovation and sustainability in this sector. 
Strengthening Biotechnology Leadership
Deepanwita Chattopadhyay was appointed as the Vice President of the Association of Biotechnology Led Enterprises (ABLE), further strengthening IKP’s leadership in advancing the biotechnology sector. 
We had the privilege of hosting remarkable visitors in 2024
During their visit to IKP in January, the German Centre for Research and Innovation - DWIH New Delhi aimed to foster strategic collaborations between Indian and German incubators, enhance cross-cultural ties, and advance entrepreneurial ecosystems.
A Mission Officer from the French Consulate in Bengaluru, accompanied by representatives from the Indo-French Chamber of Commerce and Industry, visited IKP in June  to explore ways to strengthen Indo-French collaborations in innovation and identify opportunities for technological advancements.
Swissnex and the Consulate General of Switzerland seek to enhance Indo-Swiss ties in academia and innovation. This visit in June, emphasised our shared mission of promoting research, collaboration, and sustainable development.
We showcased our dynamic ecosystem to the delegates from Bank of America in June. The discussions focused on potential collaborations to enhance health technology and foster innovation in life sciences.
Officials from the Rwanda Development Board, the Rwanda Ministry of Health, and Sathguru Management Consultants discussed collaboration opportunities in life sciences and technology during their visit to IKP in February.
The Innovators Connect-Tandem 2024 program, a collaboration between India and Germany, brought together early-career researchers and start-ups in November.
A delegation from Innovate UK visited our Genome Valley campus in June. The group included representatives from 14 leading businesses dedicated to combating AMR. During this visit, we explored opportunities for joint innovation and global collaboration, reinforcing our commitment to the One Health approach through our International One Health Incubator.
The second batch of the Copenhagen Business Immersion Program 2024 consisting of 40 students, visited IKP in November to build business models with Indian companies. IKP supported startups—Bycus Therapeutics, Huwel Lifesciences, Monitra Health, UR Advanced Therapeutics, and IKP PRIME (a technology transfer office of IKP)—collaborated with the students. It marked a significant milestone in our ongoing collaboration with Copenhagen Business School to drive innovation and global partnerships.
Inaugurating The Narayanan Vaghul Building at IKMC2024
During IKMC2024, IKP marked a momentous occasion with the inauguration of The Narayanan Vaghul Building, dedicated to the visionary founder of IKP. The event paid tribute to Mr. Vaghul's enduring legacy and his profound contributions to the organization’s success.
We forged impactful partnerships through key agreements, driving innovation and collaboration forward
IKP, in partnership with USP, launched SheLeads in Science—an initiative aimed at empowering women in STEM through mentorship, training, and networking opportunities, promoting diversity and fostering women leaders.

Deepanwita Chattopadhyay Receives Rotary Vocational Excellence Award 2024 
Deepanwita Chattopadhyay was honoured with the Rotary Vocational Excellence Award 2024 by the Rotary Club of Hyderabad Deccan. This prestigious award acknowledges her exemplary leadership, unwavering resilience, and dedication to ethical values, celebrating her outstanding contributions to IKP and the wider community.
Manish Kumar Receives RTTP Pin Recognition
Manish Kumar, Senior Manager - BD & Tech Transfer (Pharma & Medtech) at IKP PRIME (The Technology Transfer Office of IKP), was awarded the prestigious RTTP pin, honouring his exceptional expertise and dedication.
Six grant awardees supported by IKP received the 23rd Biotechnology Ignition Grant (BIG), with IKP serving as the implementation partner. They were recognized for their exceptional contributions to advancing innovative solutions in the life sciences sector.
Five startups were selected for Stage II of the Agri-Grand Challenge (AgGC) program, a joint initiative by BIRAC and IKP. These startups will receive funding of up to ₹100 lakhs to advance technology-driven solutions aimed at boosting farmers' incomes in India.  
Six startups were selected as winners of NIDHI PRAYAS Call 10. This initiative by the Department of Science and Technology funds early-stage prototypes, helping entrepreneurs turn innovative ideas into market-ready products.  
The Amrit Grand Challenge - जनCARE program accelerates digital health innovations through targeted funding. It supported 86 startups across three categories: 74 early-stage innovations received up to INR 10 lakhs each for ideation and testing; 10 late-stage startups were awarded INR 20 lakhs each for pre-commercialization; and 2 advanced-stage startups received INR 50 lakhs each for multi-centric product deployment.
The second cohort of the IKP Fellows program in 2024 travelled to Germany, Belgium, China, and Dubai, building on the success of the first cohort. In total, 12 fellows across both cohorts participated, leveraging opportunities for global exposure, networking, and market entry. We supported their endeavors through IKP Growth Labs (IGL), and travel grants of up to USD 2,500. These grants enabled them to participate in conferences and trade fairs, fostering networking opportunities and the exploration of new avenues for growth. 
IKP-supported startups, Bycus Therapeutics and Lamark Biotech, have secured funding of ₹19.3 crore and ₹2.7 crore, respectively, for  projects under National Biopharma Mission. Bycus Therapeutics, a fast-growing biotech company, is dedicated to R&D aimed at improving the affordability and quality of healthcare. Its flagship product, a complex biosimilar, Pegloticase has the potential to significantly impact global healthcare. Lamark Biotech specializes in developing thermostable biologics, including its flagship product InsulinStrong—a crystalline form of recombinant human insulin that eliminates the need for refrigeration.
