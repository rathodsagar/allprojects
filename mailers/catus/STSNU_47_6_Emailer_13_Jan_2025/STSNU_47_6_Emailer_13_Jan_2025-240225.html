Research Achievements
Graphene-Powered Laser Technology for Flexible Displays in Wearable Devices
 
Researchers at SeoulTech have designed a graphene-assisted laser lift-off technique that enables separation of ultrathin organic light-emitting diode display. By integrating graphene between the polyimide film and glass carrier, the method prevents mechanical damage and residue, making it ideal for flexible, wearable electronics. The study highlights the potential for more efficient and cost-effective manufacturing of stretchable devices.

A Novel Tool for Transforming Presentation Videos into Structured Documents
 
SeoulTech researchers have developed PV2DOC, a tool that transforms presentation videos into structured, searchable PDF documents. By integrating visual and audio data, the software generates summaries with text, images, and formulas, making video content more accessible and easier to store. This tool enhances efficiency in analyzing and managing presentation materials.

Water-Enhanced Catalytic Plastic Recycling for Sustainable Waste Management
 
Researchers at SeoulTech have revolutionized catalytic plastic recycling by introducing a water-enhanced method that improves the conversion of polyolefins—the primary contributor to plastic waste—into valuable fuels. By adding water to ruthenium-based catalysts, the process enhances efficiency, extends catalyst lifespan, and reduces operational costs. This breakthrough offers a promising solution for tackling global plastic pollution, fostering a circular economy, and improving both economic and environmental outcomes.

Boosting Efficiency and Power Output in Vibration Energy Harvesting Using Novel Electromagnetic Design
 
Researchers at SeoulTech have proposed novel designs for electromagnetic induction-type vibration energy harvesters to improve power generation efficiency and output. By optimizing magnet and coil configurations to maximize magnetic flux gradients, the study proposes strategies such as using repulsive magnet pairs, yoke structures, and enhanced coil placement. The findings demonstrate a significant increase in power output, providing a pathway for more efficient, smaller-scale energy harvesters with potential applications in wearable devices and wireless sensors.

Advancing Sustainable Solutions with Energy and Environment Research
The Energy and Environment Research Lab at SeoulTech is at the forefront
of developing next-generation energy solutions.
Focused on redox flow battery (RFB) technology and energy storage systems, the lab aims to revolutionize large-capacity, high-efficiency energy storage.
Through interdisciplinary research on RFB stack manufacturing, materials, and big data-based energy distribution, SeoulTech is driving innovations to meet global energy demands, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and pave the way for a sustainable future.
